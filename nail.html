All About The Hands and Feet

    Hands
  • When washing your hands, you should avoid using hot water as it leads to dry skin
  • Moisture daily, even after you hop out the shower
  • Wear gloves when doing dirty work (dishes, yard work, etc)
  • Apply sunscreen 15 minutes before you go outside, making sure to get both sides of your hands, and remember to reapply every two hours!
  • Clip your nails regularly
  • The first step to a proper manicure is applying a primer to help create a smooth canvas on which to apply your polish
  • Apply a base coat, a base coat can help to prevent polish from staining your nails
  • If you notice your nails feeling extra brittle or looking yellowish in between your polish changes, itâ€™s time to give them a rest (1 to 2 weeks)
  • When filing, file in one direction. Avoid going back and forth, it could lead to rough edges
  • If you notice your gel manicure starting to chip, do not peel the polish off of your nails. It could strip off your nail and leave them weak
      • Feet
  • Keep your feet clean by washing them every day in warm soapy water
  • Don't soak your feet; could destroy your skin's natural oils
  • Dry your feet after washing them, especially between the toes to avoid fungal infections
  • If your skin is dry, apply moisturising cream all over the foot, except for between the toes
  • Remove hard skin and calluses with a pumice stone or foot file
  • Trim your toenails regularly using proper nail clippers
  • Limit time wearing high heels
  • Change socks daily to avoid foot odor
  • Avoid wearing flip-flops all the time; could cause pain in the arch and heel pain
  • Never walk barefoot
  • If you have a problem with smelly feet, soak them in a mixture of vinegar and water
